📈 Time Plots — Line Chart vs Time Series vs Comparative

📈 Time Plots — 3 Types for Trends Over Time

From course: "Time plots are located in the Graphs group in the Objects pane and include comparative time series plots, line charts, and time series plots." Time plots are useful for viewing trends over time.

📈 Line Chart

"Line charts require a category and a measure."

X-axis: Any category (age, product, region)
Measures: 1 or more
Y-axes: 1 (single)
Group role: ✅ Yes
Lattice: ✅ Yes

Best for: Univariate or multivariate analysis across any category

⏱️ Time Series Plot

"Time series plots require a date or datetime data item for the Time axis and one measure."

X-axis: Date/Datetime ✅ (required)
Measures: Exactly 1
Y-axes: 1 (single)
Group role: ✅ Yes
Lattice: ❌ No

Best for: Seasonal patterns, peaks, troughs

📊 Comparative Time Series

"Comparative time series plots require a date or datetime data item for the Time axis and two measures."

X-axis: Date/Datetime ✅ (required)
Measures: Exactly 2
Y-axes: 2 (left + right, separate)
Group role: ❌ No
Lattice: ❌ No

Best for: Identifying anomalies between two series

📊 Interactive: All 3 Time Plots Compared

📈 Line Chart — any category
⏱️ Time Series Plot — date axis, 1 measure
📊 Comparative — 2 measures, 2 Y-axes
⚠️ Exam Tip: Line Chart can use ANY category (not just time). Time Series Plot specifically needs a DATE/DATETIME — it has a "Time axis" role, not a "Category" role. Comparative Time Series needs EXACTLY 2 measures — one for the left axis, one for the right.
🧠 Mnemonic: "Line = L lots of categories. Time = Takes dates, 1 measure. Comparative = Compares 2 on 2 axes."

🎯 Choose the Right One

Q: "Am I comparing two or more things over time?"
From course: "For comparisons over time, use a line chart or a time series plot."

🔹 No date? Use Line Chart — X-axis can be age groups, product lines, regions
🔹 Date + 1 measure? Use Time Series Plot — Perfect for seasonal trends
🔹 Date + 2 measures? Use Comparative Time Series — Different scales, two Y-axes
