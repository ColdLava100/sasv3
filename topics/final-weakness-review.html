📖 IN-DEPTH: Logarithmic Scale

What is a log scale?
Instead of marking numbers evenly (1, 2, 3, 4...), a log scale marks powers of 10 (1, 10, 100, 1000, 10000...). The distance from 1→10 equals the distance from 10→100 equals the distance from 100→1000.

Why use it? When data has extreme outliers — e.g., most salaries are 30k-200k but CEO makes 50 million. On a normal scale the CEO bar fills the page and everyone else looks tiny. On a log scale, 30k and 50M can both be seen clearly.

What does it preserve? Order of observations (ranking) — bigger values still appear higher. It does NOT preserve exact visual proportions — 1000 is NOT drawn 10x bigger than 100, they're the same distance apart.

🧠 Memory: "Log = Large gaps. Use when Lopsided data. Preserves Order, not Original values."

📖 IN-DEPTH: P-Spline vs Linear vs Quadratic

The 3 Best Fit line models:

1. Linear — The line is a STRAIGHT line. Y = a + bX. Simple, interpretable. Use when relationship is consistently increasing or decreasing (e.g., age → salary).
🧠 "Linear = Line. A straight ruler 📏."

2. Quadratic — The line curves once (U-shape or ∩-shape). Y = a + bX + cX². Use when there's a peak or valley (e.g., age → happiness: low in 20s, high in 40s, low in 70s).
🧠 "Quadratic = Quarter turn (one bend) 🔄."

3. P-Spline (Penalized B-Spline) — The line can wiggle multiple times to follow complex patterns. It's a flexible, data-driven curve. Use when the relationship is complicated and you don't want to force it into a simple shape.
🧠 "P-Spline = Pretzel 🥨 — it bends many times to follow the data. 'Penalized' means it's punished for over-complicating (avoids overfitting)."

❌ Why NOT Cubic? Cubic (X³) is NOT a standard option in SAS VA's Best Fit line. The choices are Linear, Quadratic, and P-Spline only.

🧠 Memory: "Line = straight. Quad = one bend. P-Spline = many bends. Cubic = Cut (not available)."
